## The Voice Model — Top-Down Cascade (LOCKED 2026-06-10)

Full canonical version: Messaging Standards (Confluence TS1, page 96534539). This is the
structural model behind every Bookmarked communication. Work from the top, descend fast into
the practical. Brief at the top, heavy at the bottom.

**Four layers:** (1) The Why — one breath, the mission. (2) The Liability Frame — SB 13/SB 412
put the decision on leadership's desk; sells up to the buyer. (2.5) The Engine — Book Intelligence,
we understand books, not just list them. (3) The Product — most of the weight; a named moment
on a real screen in OnShelf.

**Book Intelligence = two signals:** outside-in (what the world says about a title — context,
never a verdict) + inside-out (how widely districts already trust it — aggregate, the moat).

**Four locked rules:** (1) Language Boundary — describe what's known ABOUT books, never what's
INSIDE. (2) Positive framing — lead with gain, name pain once. (3) **Network Privacy** —
network signal always aggregate ("widely held"), never name a district, never imply exposure;
intelligence flows toward the district, never reveals it. (4) De-emphasize challenges/bans —
one signal, never the headline; we're a Book Intelligence company, not a ban-protection company.

**Audience: sell up, honor sideways, never punt down.** Sell to superintendents/administrators
(they own the liability). Earn trust with librarians (tool that makes them better, never
replacement). Never let the super punt the decision down to the library. **Dual-address:** write
so the superintendent feels protected, the librarian feels respected and equipped, the
administrator feels informed — all reading it as theirs. Avoid possessives like "your librarians."

**Continuity rule (for installed-base comms):** Existing districts already use OnShelf. Never
imply OnShelf is new to them or that they're "getting access" for the first time. The story is
enhancement, not introduction — the same platform they know, dramatically improved based on
librarian and district feedback. Frame the upgrade as something built *for* them because they
asked, never something happening *to* them.

**Single sender model (LOCKED 2026-06-10):** Every district-facing email sends from
`support@bookmarked.com`, never an individual's address. A person/agent may be named in the
body or footer for warmth (Patrick, Bryan, Houston the CS agent, or Steve as founder), but the
from-line is always support@. This is why there's no "unknown sender" trust gap — districts
never see an unfamiliar personal address.

> Full authoring record (reasoning, reference cut, every Steve-driven decision):
> `references/voice-model-authoring-2026-06.md`. Read it before revising the voice model.

**Reviewing teammates' content (Steve's rule — LOCKED):** when reviewing anyone's Confluence
or GitHub content, NEVER edit the original body. Layer feedback non-destructively: inline
comments anchored to the exact text (GitHub PR review comments / Confluence inline comments)
plus one companion review page linked from the original. Preserve the original; our edits
live as a layer on top.

**Minimal-edit / surgical-rewrite discipline (Steve's rule — LOCKED 2026-06-10):** when
fixing a teammate's draft, change ONLY what breaks a rule. Do not "improve" surrounding prose.
Steve repeatedly pulls back overcorrection — the instinct to rewrite a whole sentence when one
word is wrong reads as not respecting the author's work. Process: (1) isolate the exact rule
violation, (2) propose the smallest swap that fixes it, (3) leave everything else verbatim.
When you over-rewrite, expect "go back to their content, just tweak that one line." Present
suggestions as before/after tables with the original line intact so the author sees exactly
what changes and what doesn't. The suggestion is a recommendation on wording, never a mandate
("that's my suggestion on how we word it, not saying say it exactly like that").

**Show before you post (Steve's rule):** never post review comments or send drafted comms
without showing Steve the exact content first — before/after for each change, with reasoning.
He wants to verify, not take your word. Show a few representative examples for "flavor," let
him edit, then run the rest in the approved style.

**Installed-base = existing users, name the enhancement (learned 2026-06-10):** closeout/sunset
emails (e.g. M+44) that say "your transition is complete" land on a customer who may not even
know anything changed — they just log in and the screen looks different. Always (a) say the
OLDER version is closed (not "your access is retired," which is muddy since they're still in
OnShelf), and (b) name what they'll SEE now — the enhanced OnShelf, the new features. The
"here's what's new when you log in" message belongs at GO-LIVE, not at the closeout. Flag
sequencing if the enhanced UI is first named 44 days in.

## The Four Pillars

Every customer communication anchors to these four points. You don't have to use
all four in every email — but the overall journey must deliver all four.

1. **Significant improvements** — new capabilities around book ordering, enhanced
   book intelligence, better tools for managing the library program across campuses
2. **Platform move** — as of [date], your district moves to OnShelf
   so you have access to everything new
3. **Walk-through offer** — we'd love to set up a time to walk you through what's new
   and how it benefits your district
4. **Extended access** — you have access through September 1, 2026 to explore at your
   own pace; your current agreement renews on [date]

| Product | **OnShelf** | the platform districts log into. The hero name. |

### OnShelf Engage — Present But Buried (LOCKED 2026-06-10)
Engage (parent see/restrict/allow) exists but is **deliberately NOT marketed**. It

Destiny for restriction (built-in). **We do not compete with Destiny on restriction.**
Keep Engage discoverable for districts who ask; never make it a headline or selling
point. Lead capability is always OnShelf Review.

## Hard Language Rules

### Never use these in district-facing content:
- **"migration"** — internal word only
- **"V1"** or **"V2"** — internal labels only
- **"sunsetting"** — implies abandonment
- **"old system"** — condescending
- **Em dashes** — dead AI tell, hard ban on all customer content

### Always use:
- "OnShelf" (product) / "OnShelf Review" (lead capability)
- "enhanced platform" / "major product update"
- "new capabilities"
- "we built this for you" / "excited to show you"
- Language that implies good news, not administrative disruption

## Tone

**Warm. Confident. Peer-to-peer.**

School administrators are busy, trust-sensitive, and FOIA-aware. Every email we
send could be read by a board member, a parent, or a reporter. Write accordingly.

- Never urgent. Never apologetic about the product.
- Own delays or gaps on "we" — never imply the district is behind.
- Short. One ask per email. The ask is near the end.
- No pleasantries before the point.
- Specific details (names, dates, counts) over generic language.

## Pitfalls

- **Canon drift on product name (discovered 2026-06-10)** — this skill and the
  migration comms were built on "OnShelf Review and Engage," but the canonical Product
  Vision names the product **OnShelf** with "OnShelf Review" as one module. Comms got
  built in the CS/migration corner without syncing to the locked Marketing brand layer.
  Lesson: when doing brand/voice work, START from `references/canonical-brand-voice.md`
  and the live Confluence canon, not from downstream comms skills. One company, one
  voice means the WORDS have a single source of truth, not just the sender identity.
- **E7 (48-hr check-in) — body says "walkthrough of V2"** (one hit). Swap "V2" → "OnShelf".
  Also its pre-send note / personalization checklist leak internal "migration-tracker" framing.
- **E9 (M+37) — THREE customer-facing V1 references** (not two): "the original version of
  OnShelf (V1)", "that access ends", "After [DATE], V1 will no longer be accessible." Plus an
  em-dash. Rewrite to "your previous OnShelf access" / "that older version" and name the
  enhanced OnShelf they're on now.
- **E10 (M+44) — most violation-dense template:** "migration" ×2 ("your migration is fully
  closed", "smooth migration") PLUS "V1" and "V2" in the opener. Fix: "older version is fully
  closed", "running entirely on the enhanced OnShelf [name the new features]", "transition is
  complete and verified."
- **E8 (M+7, was unflagged) — also full of internal language:** "Migration status: Clean",
  "your migration is verified and closed", "V1 will go fully offline." Scrub all three. SEPARATE
  judgment flag for Patrick (not a unilateral rewrite): the loudest element is a 5-line
  data-verification table — frames the moment as "did the data move correctly?" (our concern)
  when at 7 days the district's moment is "is my team using it?" Consider leading with adoption,
  demoting counts to the attachment. Owner's call.
- **Single-sender model RESOLVES the old "Houston not introduced" trust gap** — all sends are
  from support@bookmarked.com, so districts never see an unfamiliar personal address. Houston is
  a CS agent who may be named in a body for warmth, never a from-line. (Also resolves the E8
  "from Bryan vs. signed Patrick" ownership conflict — it's support@ with whatever name fits.)
- **Sales Intro Email (E1) is a gap** — as of June 2026, doesn't exist in repo. It's the front
  door; everything downstream assumes it was sent. Steve decided E1 comes from HIM (the founder)
  via support@, named in the footer ("Steve Wandler, Founder, Bookmarked"). Register is warm
  founder-to-existing-happy-customer, NOT the full liability cascade (installed base, not cold
  prospects). Credits district/librarian feedback as the reason for the upgrade. Draft lives in
  `references/voice-model-authoring-2026-06.md`. Decide: tease specific features or stay
  high-level (Steve's lean: light), and whether founder-signed goes to ALL districts or only
  where his name carries weight (templated risk if every wave gets it).
- **Operational migration emails stay LIGHT — do NOT force the liability cascade into them**
  (Steve + advisor agreed 2026-06-10). SB 13/SB 412 framing in a "your upgrade is tomorrow"
  email reads as fear-mongering and trips Positive Framing. The locked voice cascade belongs at
  the FRONT of the funnel (E1, sales decks, website), not mid-sequence operational reminders.
