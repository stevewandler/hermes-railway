## The Two-Buckets Rule (most important concept)

Budget-stressed districts only fund two things:
1. **Legal/compliance mandates** — they fund it because the law forces it
2. **Hard-cash reductions** — headcount eliminated, real money out

**"Value" and "time savings" do not clear.** Giving a librarian 10 hours back is a value saving, not a money saving, unless those hours convert to a cut position or avoided hire. Always translate the pitch into cash or compliance language. Never lead with time savings.

*Source: Michael Ruiz, Director of Library Services, Garland ISD — a deficit-year buyer.*

## IMTA — The Third Door

IMTA (Instructional Materials and Technology Allotment) bypasses both general-fund buckets entirely. It is a **separate, earmarked state account** that cannot be spent on anything except instructional materials and educational technology.

Key facts:
- **Every Texas district gets it** — formula-based, $174.69/student for 2026-27 biennium
- **Funds do not expire** — they roll over year to year ("IMTA bank")
- **Bookmarked is IMTA-eligible** under two TEA categories: (1) software that manages/accesses instructional materials, (2) software that analyzes suitability of instructional materials for TEKS
- **A district's general fund deficit does not touch IMTA** — completely separate accounts
- **No RFP required** through ESC 16 fiscal agent — clean procurement path
- **Districts can use IMTA for the platform AND the books** flowing through it

**The IMTA pitch frame:** "You have $X in instructional materials funds that can only be spent on classroom technology. Bookmarked qualifies. Let's talk procurement, not budget."

**Finding district IMTA balances:**
- TEA Dec 2025 Legislative Report Appendix A — first-ever public dataset of all TX district FY2025 balances
- PEIMS Financial Data (Fund Code 420) — annual, lagged ~6 months
- TEA IMTA Allotment Reports — annual allotments (not cumulative balance)
- TEA 2026-27 biennial allotment PDF (direct): https://tea.texas.gov/academics/instructional-materials/procure-instructional-materials-in-emat/2026-2027-imta-funds.pdf — searchable by district name, shows biennial total (divide by 2 for annual)
- URL: https://tea.texas.gov/about-tea/government-relations/legislative-reports/imta-balance-report-dec-2025
- **Jan Gilboy** can look up current EMAT balances — she confirmed Garland's $2.17M unspent from the live TEA EMAT report (June 2026). She is the internal source for current-cycle balances before they appear in public datasets.
- **Bookmarked Confluence** (Sales Hub space) has a dedicated IMTA page with talk track, eligibility case, spend patterns, and district intelligence portal: https://bookmarked-team.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SH/pages/147488769/IMTA+Funding

**IMTA vs IMRA distinction:**
- IMTA = flexible district allotment (Bookmarked eligible)
- IMRA = state-held account for TEA-adopted materials only (Bookmarked not directly eligible, but IMRA-purchased books can flow through Bookmarked's workflow)
- Always pitch IMTA, not IMRA

**Garland ISD:** $2.17M unspent IMTA confirmed by Jan Gilboy (TEA EMAT report, June 2026). Biennial allotment is $9.2M.

## The Two-Track Relationship Model

When a champion is both a buyer AND a community leader (e.g. Michael Ruiz = Garland ISD buyer + NTX TALAS Founding President), run two tracks simultaneously:

**Track 1 — Personal/operational:** Make their individual life easier. Build their AI setup. Be their accountability partner. This has to work first. When they experience personal value, they sell the org internally without you having to pitch anyone.

**Track 2 — Organizational:** Help them build something for their organization (TALAS chapter, district initiative). Co-develop it. Their name on it. Makes them look like a strategic leader. Opens doors to their network.

**The cascade:** Track 1 working → champion tells board → board wants in → you do Track 1 for each board member → board tells members → membership grows.

**Critical rule:** Never flip the sequence. Don't pitch the org until the champion has experienced personal value. Don't mention Bookmarked in TALAS conversations — let it come up naturally.

## Champion-First Selling

- The champion's job is to carry the deal upward (Asst. Supt → Supt → cabinet/board)
- Whatever you build must be **ammunition they can carry up** in finance's language
- Build the internal-sell artifact first, not as an afterthought
- Know the approval chain: who approves the dollar, in which budget bucket, what format they need

**Procurement thresholds (Texas, typical):**
- Under $25-50K: department director / superintendent discretion (no board vote)
- $50-150K: superintendent approval, competitive quotes
- Over $150K: board approval, formal competitive bidding

Price below $50K where possible to keep deals at the champion level.

## The Garland Flagship Structure (recommended play)

For deficit-year districts with IMTA availability:

1. **Anchor on compliance** (urgency + own budget bucket) — SB13/HB900/SB412
2. **Justify on IMTA** (removes the budget wall entirely)
3. **De-risk with results-contingent entry** (small credited number now; full number triggers on proven savings)
4. **Wrap in founding-partner terms** — special pricing in exchange for public reference + network access

---

## Open Questions to Always Resolve Before Designing an Offer

1. Is the IMTA balance confirmed and current?
2. What is the district's vendor PO threshold (board vs. admin approval)?
3. Is the champion the final dollar, or do they sell upward?
4. What is the compliance exposure (SB13/HB900/SB412 audit risk)?
5. What is the annual library materials budget? (Often larger than labor savings)

## IMTA Email Framing (from Garland close, June 2026)

When sending the IMTA email, the frame is: "you have funds that can only be spent on this — let's talk procurement, not budget." Keep it to 4-5 sentences. Don't reference the expired proposal or previous pricing. Don't apologize for the gap. Just surface the funding path and ask if it's worth a conversation.

Subject: "Good news on the Garland funding question" — specific, positive, no urgency pressure.

Never combine the IMTA email with the TALAS/relationship email. Send them as separate threads on the same day. The IMTA is a Bookmarked conversation; TALAS is a personal relationship investment. Mixing them muddles both.

## Pitfalls

- **Don't lead with time savings** — translate everything to cash or compliance first
- **Don't design the offer before resolving the five questions above** — especially the IMTA balance and PO threshold
- **Don't make a flagship district the lab rat for a company pricing pivot** — separate "close this district" from "design our pricing model"
- **Freemium gets "try," not "pay"** — conversion still hits the budget wall. Don't ship a free motion without a funded-bucket conversion story
- **Per-student pricing shrinks with enrollment decline** — strong case to move to per-user/per-campus for deficit districts losing students
- **When selling THROUGH ESC channels, do NOT lead with compliance** — ESC contacts (especially library services people) are educators, not procurement officers. They'll shut down if the first thing out of your mouth is SB13. Lead with library vitality and Book Intelligence — what the library of the future looks like, how AI helps librarians understand their collections so the right kid finds the right book. Compliance is what runs underneath and gives them their time back, not the headline. Steve's own framing: "Compliance goes away when you're getting the right book."
