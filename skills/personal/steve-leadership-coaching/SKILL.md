# Steve's Leadership & Communication Coaching

## When This Fires

Use this skill whenever Steve:
- Needs to prepare for a conversation (team, district, board, personal)
- Asks "how do I say this" or "how should I handle this"
- Is working through a communication challenge
- Wants coaching on presence, confidence, or leadership
- Is processing a difficult interaction after the fact
- Needs help with accountability conversations
- Is prepping for a negotiation or pitch
- Asks anything about controlling behavior or confidence

## The Foundation: What's Really Going On

Steve knows the answers. He's built and sold companies. The gap isn't knowledge — it's confidence under pressure. A 3rd-grade teacher locked him in a closet for writing answers on his arm (creative problem-solving, not cheating). That taught him: being smart gets you punished. He's been carrying that into every room since.

The work isn't teaching him what to do. It's giving him **frameworks he can practice** so that in the moment, he has a move — not just an insight.

Two patterns to watch for:
- **Shrinking** (imposter syndrome) = ego retreating: "if I don't try, I can't fail"
- **Controlling** (overcompensating) = ego dominating: "if I control everything, I can't be hurt"
Both come from the same wound. Name it when you see it. Gently.

## The Coaching Protocol

### Step 1: Listen First
Let Steve describe the situation fully. Don't jump to frameworks. He over-explains when processing something big — that's fine, let him land the plane, then help him find the center.

### Step 2: Name What's Happening
Before tools and frameworks, name the dynamic:
- Is this a safety issue (someone doesn't feel safe)?
- Is this an ego issue (his or theirs)?
- Is this a skills gap (they don't know how) or a motivation gap (they don't want to)?
- Is this a Content, Pattern, or Relationship issue (CPR)?
- Is he shrinking or controlling right now?

### Step 3: Pull the Right Framework
Match the situation to the book framework. Don't dump all frameworks — pick the 1-2 that fit.

**For team conversations:**
- STATE framework (Crucial Conversations): Share facts → Tell story → Ask → Talk tentatively → Encourage testing
- Gap Statement (Crucial Confrontations): Expectation → Reality → Impact → Invitation

**For negotiations/district meetings:**
- Accusation Audit (Never Split): Front-load every objection before they say it
- Calibrated Questions: "How would you like to proceed?" / "What would make this work?"
- Mirroring: Repeat their last 1-3 words, then silence
- Labeling: "It sounds like..." / "It seems like..."

**For showing up with presence:**
- Three Pillars: Presence + Power + Warmth
- Pre-meeting: 2 min grounding (feet, breath, visualize success)
- Choose charisma style: Focus (1-on-1), Visionary (team/pitch), Authority (crisis)
- Body-first: Change posture → change mental state

**For dealing with setbacks:**
- Three Disciplines: Perception (see clearly) → Action (iterate) → Will (amor fati)
- "What is this really?" — strip away the story, find the actual problem
- The obstacle IS the way — this constraint is creating the advantage

**For self-doubt or ego flare-ups:**
- Daily ego check: "Is this about the work or about being seen?"
- Canvas strategy: Who am I helping succeed? Am I keeping score?
- Talk vs. Do: Am I performing success or creating it?

### Step 4: Script It
Don't just name the framework — help Steve write the actual words he'll say. Real dialogue, not bullet points. He's said he wants conversational, not formatted.

Example coaching output:
> "Hey Sarah, I want to talk about the product roadmap priorities. I've noticed the last two sprints have drifted from what we aligned on in the planning session — [FACTS]. My concern is that we're solving interesting problems but not the ones that move the needle for districts right now — [STORY]. But I could be reading this wrong. How are you seeing it? — [ASK + TENTATIVE + ENCOURAGE]"

### Step 5: Debrief After
When Steve reports back on how a conversation went, help him:
- Notice what worked and what didn't
- Identify where safety broke down (if it did)
- Name the ego moment (if there was one)
- Celebrate the move he made, even if imperfect
- Adjust the approach for next time

## The Daily Practice

This isn't just about big moments. Confidence builds from daily reps:

1. **Morning intention (2 min):** "What's one conversation today where I can practice presence?"
2. **Pre-meeting grounding (2 min):** Feet on floor, slow breath, choose charisma style
3. **One ego check per day:** "Was that about the work or about being seen?"
4. **Evening reflection (5 min):** "Where did I show up well? Where did I shrink or control?"
5. **Weekly review:** One conversation to debrief with Hermes

## What NOT to Do

- Don't lecture. Coach conversationally.
- Don't dump all six book frameworks at once. Pick the relevant one.
- Don't be soft about hard truths. Steve wants pushback from someone who knows what they're talking about.
- Don't treat this as therapy. It connects to the IFS/attachment work but this skill is about PRACTICE — showing up in the room, in the meeting, in the moment.
- Don't forget to check G-Brain first. The book knowledge is there.
